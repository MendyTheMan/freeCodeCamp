 <div id="img-caption">Famous Picture of The Rebbe Rashab</div>

<div id="tribute-info">
  <ul>
    <li><b>1860:</b> Birth of the Rebbe Rashab on the 20th of the Jewish month of Cheshvan (the 5th of November).</li>
    <li><b>1875:</b> Married his cousin Rebbetzin Shterna Sara after the close of Shabbat, on the 11th of the Jewish month of Elul (the 11th of September).</li>
    <li><b>1880:</b> Begins his work in public affairs.</li>
    <li><b>1882:</b> Begins guiding the chassidim, and accepts the post of rebbe.</li>
    <li><b>1892:</b> Endeavors to have the expulsion from Moscow rescinded, and effects a postponement until the summer; provides substantial financial subsidies to enable those expelled to settle in new places.</li>
    <li><b>1893:</b> Reestablishes the committee of communal leaders in Petersburg.</li>
    <li><b>1894:</b> Visits the settlements in Kherson province.</li>
    <li><b>1897:</b> Establishes the Yeshivot Tomchei T’mimim.</li>
    <li><b>1898:</b> Travels in the public interest to Kiev and Odessa.</li>
    <li><b>1899:</b> Expresses his negative views regarding the Zionist party.</li>
    <li><b>1901:</b> Visits Germany, France and Holland on communal concerns.</li>
    <li><b>1902:</b> With the assistance of the philanthropic brothers Poliakov and the help of the I.C.A., establishes a factory for weaving and spinning wool in Dubrovna, Mogilev province. Some 2000 Jews earn ample livelihoods working there.</li>
    <li><b>1904:</b> Establishes committees to send matzot to Jewish soldiers in the Far East.</li>
    <li><b>1906:</b> Successfully endeavors in Petersburg to halt the pogroms.</li>
    <li><b>1907:</b> Prepares a detailed program entitled Hitagdut Hayerei’im (“The Union of Observant Jews”), and conveys it to Rabbi Breuer and the learned, G‑d-fearing Rabbi Yaakov Rosenheim.</li>
    <li><b>1908:</b> Participates in the meeting of communal leaders of Germany in Berlin.</li>
    <li><b>1909:</b> At the Katowitz Conference he announces his withdrawal from the Agudat Yisrael.</li>
    <li><b>1911:</b> Establishes Yeshivat Torat Emet in Hebron, Israel.</li>
    <li><b>1915:</b> On the 17th of the Jewish month of Cheshvan (the 25th of October), he leaves Lubavitch and moves to Rostov, on the River Don.</li>
    <li><b>1916:</b> During the summer, establishes Yeshivot in Georgia.</li>
    <li><b>1917:</b> Succeeds in securing legal exemption from military service for 2,382 religious functionaries (rabbis, cantors, ritual slaughterers, etc.).</li>
    <li><b>1920:</b> After Shabbat ends, of the Torah reading of Vayikra, eve of Sunday, 2 Nissan (the 21st of March), at 3:30 AM, he passes away and is interred in Rostov, on the river Don.</li>
    
  </ul>
</div>
